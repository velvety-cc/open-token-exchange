## One-paragraph verdict

An Open Token Exchange is **buildable but the underlying is not a true speculatable commodity**.
OpenRouter lists provider prices with **no per-token markup**, so an oracle reading it tracks
providers cleanly — but prices are **administratively set, multi-dimensional, and move one
direction** (a ~40× decline 2023–2025), not the bidirectional volatility speculation needs. The
strongest precedents are **2026 regulated compute futures** (CME+Silicon Data, ICE+Ornn) and a
**March 2026 SIT paper**, all showing a fragmented non-deliverable compute commodity can be
cash-settled against an independent daily index. **Hyperliquid HIP‑3** makes a synthetic perp
feasible, but *deployer-owns-the-oracle* concentrates value and manipulation risk in the feed. The
defensible MVP is a **cash-settled instrument or a physically-settled voucher marketplace anchored
to a transparent multi-provider daily token-price index**; a HIP‑3 perp is fastest but carries
oracle-trust and US regulatory risk. **Drop the arbitrage framing** — tokens are consumed, not
resold.

<Note>
**Killed claim (0–3):** that HIP‑3 *requires* a "manipulation-resistant underlying with genuine
economic significance." The docs show no such governance gate — the **500k HYPE stake is the only
barrier**, an economic one, not an editorial one. This *helps* feasibility but *worsens* the trust
story (anyone can list anything; nothing vets the feed).
</Note>

## What the research did NOT settle

<Warning>
Four gaps the build plan must close — these drive the [Open Questions](/architecture/open-questions):

1. **Short-horizon volatility & reprice frequency** of OpenRouter per-model prices (the make-or-break
   input for a tradeable vs hedging-only product). Not measured by any surviving claim.
2. **US regulatory treatment** (CFTC jurisdiction, swap vs future, prediction-market precedent). The
   question asked for it; no verified claim covered it conclusively.
3. **Oracle manipulation-resistance** when the underlying is sourced from essentially one venue.
4. **Demand-side willingness-to-pay** — which segment actually buys, and which instrument.
</Warning>
