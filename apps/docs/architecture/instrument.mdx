<Note>
**This is the decision that resolved the project.** Rather than choosing between a *physical voucher*
and a *cash-settled derivative*, OTE's instrument is **both at once**: a prepaid, transferable,
redeemable token-voucher. Physical redeemability anchors price; a secondary market provides
liquidity and speculation. Resolved in the `/grill-me` session — see
[Open Questions](/architecture/open-questions).
</Note>

## What a voucher is

A **standardized, transferable, on-chain claim** on `N` tokens of a model (curated whitelist) or a
**frontier basket**, prepaid at a locked rate below spot, expiring at date `D`. The holder has two
exits:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
<Card title="Redeem" icon="bolt">
Route real inference through OTE and consume the tokens at the **locked price**, regardless of where
spot has moved. This is the *physical anchor* — the voucher is backed by a real, usable good.
</Card>
<Card title="Sell" icon="arrow-right-arrow-left">
Transfer the voucher on a **secondary DEX market** before consuming. Exit early, take liquidity, or
speculate. This is where two-sided trading lives — without a synthetic perp.
</Card>
</CardGroup>

This is exactly how **deliverable commodity futures** behave: physically deliverable, but most
holders close out on the exchange before delivery. Deliverability is what *anchors* the price; the
exchange is what provides liquidity.

## Why redeemability dissolves the oracle problem

The research's central warning was that **cash settlement makes the index the manipulation surface**
([Oracles & Settlement](/research/oracles-and-settlement)). A redeemable voucher **defuses this by
arbitrage**, not by trusting a feed:

<Steps>
<Step title="If the voucher trades below delivery value">
Anyone can **buy it cheap and redeem for real tokens** worth more. That buying pressure pushes the
market price back up.
</Step>
<Step title="If it trades above delivery value">
Nobody overpays when they can buy inference directly. Price falls back.
</Step>
<Step title="Result">
The market price stays **tethered to the real cost of inference automatically** — because the good is
redeemable, not because an oracle declares a number. The index becomes a **reference/UX convenience**,
not the thing money depends on.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Check>
**Consequence:** OTE needs only a *transparent reference index* for the MVP, **not** a trustless,
manipulation-proof settlement oracle. The hardened-oracle problem (and the HIP‑3 deployer-owns-the-
feed risk) only returns if OTE later adds a **non-redeemable derivative** layer.
</Check>

## The constraint you can't engineer away

<Warning>
**Tokens can't be inventoried — they're consumed at generation** ([Underlying](/research/underlying-asset)).
OTE *cannot* pre-buy tokens to warehouse against vouchers. On redemption, OTE sources inference at
**that moment's spot price**. If a voucher sold at $8/M and spot is $12/M at redemption, OTE eats the
$4. That is the **underwriting short** OTE carries — the reason the
[counterparty lay-off](/architecture/recommendation) (provider deals → speculator market) exists.
The voucher packages this risk cleanly; it does not remove it.
</Warning>

## Voucher economics (worked example)

Spot GPT‑5 output = **$10/M**. Buyer expects to burn **600M tokens / 6 months**. OTE sells a voucher
at **$8/M** (below spot — the expected decline, passed through).

| Scenario at redemption | Buyer | OTE |
|---|---|---|
| Spot falls to **$6** | Redeems & consumes at $8 (overpaid vs market, but had certainty) — or sells voucher ~$6 | Sources at $6, collected $8 → **+$2/M** |
| Spot flat at **$10** | Saved $2/M vs market | Sources at $10, collected $8 → **−$2/M**, offset by float/premium |
| Spot **resets up to $14** (new frontier model) | Protected — redeems at $8, or sells voucher ~$14 | Sources at $14, collected $8 → **−$6/M** (the tail risk) |

<Info>
OTE's **edge** is the secular decline (most scenarios resemble row 1). OTE's **risk** is an upward
**reset** (row 3) — which is why [position limits, curation, reserves, and lay-off](/architecture/recommendation)
matter, and why the [Phase‑0 study](/architecture/price-index) measures *reset frequency/size*, not
day-trading volatility.
</Info>

## Collateralization — a first-class requirement

<Warning>
Because the voucher is an **on-chain promise to deliver inference** and tokens can't be pre-bought,
holders need assurance OTE can honor redemption. The MVP must define a **reserve / collateral model**:
how much capital backs outstanding vouchers, where it's held, and what happens on an upward reset
that exceeds reserves. This is tracked as a live open question (Q12) in
[Open Questions](/architecture/open-questions).
</Warning>

## How "crypto" re-enters — healthily

<Tip>
The original instinct to use Hyperliquid / a DEX was right, but the *shape* changed. Crypto returns
as a **spot market in asset-backed, redeemable vouchers** (fungible tokens, transferable,
collateralized) — **not** a synthetic perp on a manipulable index. An asset-backed spot token is far
more defensible than a bet on a number OTE controls. A HIP‑3 perp, if ever built, sits *on top* of a
market that already has honest, redemption-anchored prices. See [HIP‑3 path](/architecture/hip3-perp).
</Tip>

## Data model (sketch)

```ts
// packages/voucher-core — the redeemable claim
type Voucher = {
  id: string;                 // on-chain token id
  underlying:                 // what it delivers
    | { kind: "model"; model: string }        // curated whitelist, e.g. "anthropic/claude-sonnet-4.x"
    | { kind: "basket"; basket: "frontier" }; // diversified class
  tokens: number;             // N tokens of headroom remaining
  lockedUsdPerM: number;      // prepaid rate, below spot at mint
  expiry: string;             // ISO8601 — use-or-settle by D
  collateralRef: string;      // reserve backing this claim
  state: "active" | "redeeming" | "expired" | "transferred";
};
```
