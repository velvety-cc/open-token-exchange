<Card title="Recommendation" icon="flag-checkered">
Ship **prepaid, on-chain, redeemable token-vouchers** ([the core instrument](/architecture/instrument))
on a **curated set of high-volume models + a frontier basket**. **OTE underwrites the forward short
itself** at first (the ~40× secular decline is the edge), then **lays risk off** to provider
committed-use deals and, later, a speculator market. Position OTE as a **neutral financial layer
across aggregators**. Drop "arbitrage" from all messaging.
</Card>

## The throughline

<Note>
**Redeemability is the product** — not the index. Because every voucher is redeemable for real
inference, its market price is anchored by arbitrage, so OTE doesn't need a trustless settlement
oracle to be honest ([why](/architecture/instrument#why-redeemability-dissolves-the-oracle-problem)).
That single property is what lets the MVP be **on-chain and tradeable from day one** while keeping
the manipulation and (much of the) regulatory risk off the table.
</Note>

## Why this sequence

The research's two real risks were (1) **the index as manipulation surface** and (2) **the underlying
may lack tradeable volatility**. The voucher design **neutralizes #1 via redeemability**, and the
phased lay-off **contains #2** by not depending on bidirectional volatility — OTE profits from the
*secular decline*, not from two-way price action.

<Steps>
<Step title="Phase 0 — Index + reset study (weeks 1–4)">
Build the **OTE reference index** (multi-source, transparent) and — more importantly — measure what an
*underwriter* needs: the **forecastability of the decline** and the **frequency & size of upward
resets** (frontier launches, provider hikes). This sizes the discount OTE can offer and the reserves
it must hold. See [Price Index](/architecture/price-index).

<Check>This is the experiment: if upward resets are frequent and large, underwriting is dangerous and the discount must shrink — learned cheaply, before minting a single voucher.</Check>
</Step>
<Step title="Phase 1 — Mint & redeem vouchers (OTE underwrites)">
Mint prepaid, collateralized, on-chain vouchers on a **curated model whitelist + frontier basket**.
Holders **redeem** for inference (routed via OpenRouter / BYOK, later direct providers). **OTE holds
the short**, backed by a defined **reserve/collateral model** (Q12). Proves willingness-to-pay and
that redemption economics work after the [fee layer](/architecture/instrument).
</Step>
<Step title="Phase 1.5 — Secondary market (liquidity & exit)">
List vouchers on a **secondary DEX/order book** so holders can sell before consuming. This is where
two-sided trading and price discovery appear — **on asset-backed spot tokens, not a synthetic perp.**
Redemption arbitrage keeps the market honest.
</Step>
<Step title="Phase 2 — Lay off the short to providers">
Negotiate **committed-use discounts** with providers (or via OpenRouter), converting OTE's
naked short into a back-to-back position. Reduces balance-sheet risk and improves the discount OTE
can pass to buyers. Strengthens the **neutral-across-aggregators** moat.
</Step>
<Step title="Phase 3 — Speculator market / optional perp">
Open the other side of the trade to speculators so hedgers' risk is absorbed by the market, not OTE.
A HIP‑3 perp **on top of the redemption-anchored spot market** is possible here (Pyth/RedStone
oracle, geofenced) — but only if it adds liquidity the spot market lacks. See
[HIP‑3 path](/architecture/hip3-perp). Whether to build it at all is Q6.
</Step>
</Steps>

## The MVP in one screen

<Card title="OTE v0 — Redeemable Token-Vouchers" icon="ticket">
**For** AI teams burning meaningful inference · **they** prepay tomorrow's lower price and stay liquid.

- Pick a **model** (curated whitelist) or the **frontier basket**.
- See OTE's **reference index** spot + the **voucher price** (below spot — the decline, passed through).
- **Prepay** for `N` tokens at the locked rate → receive an **on-chain, collateralized voucher**.
- **Redeem** anytime for real inference (routed under the hood), **or sell** the voucher on the
  secondary market to exit early.
- OTE underwrites delivery, backed by posted **reserves**.
</Card>

## What the MVP must prove (kill-criteria)

<Warning>
Kill or pivot if:

1. **Upward resets are too frequent/large** — if the underwriting short blows through reserves on
   realistic reset scenarios, OTE can't safely be the house and must wait for lay-off channels first.
2. **No willingness-to-pay** — if cost-conscious developers won't prepay for certainty + a discount,
   the committed-use thesis is wrong.
3. **Redemption economics don't close** — if the all-in fee layer (5.5% / BYOK 5%, or direct rates)
   eats the discount, the voucher has no value over buying spot.
4. **Collateral model can't earn trust** — if holders won't believe redemption is backed, the on-chain
   voucher has no secondary-market bid.
5. **OpenRouter ToS** blocks reselling/fulfillment **and** no direct-provider fallback is reachable.
</Warning>

## Decision summary (from the grill)

| Decision | Resolved to |
|---|---|
| North star | Hedging venue that earns the right to become an exchange (both, sequenced) |
| Core instrument | **Prepaid, on-chain, redeemable token-voucher** (physical anchor + tradeable) |
| First customer | Committed-use buyer ("Reserved Tokens") on OpenRouter's high-burn tail |
| Contract unit | **Frontier basket + per-model curated whitelist** (combo) |
| Counterparty | OTE underwrites first → providers → speculators (hybrid lay-off) |
| Settlement | **Physical redemption** (anchors price) + **secondary-market exit** |
| Index role | **Reference only** (redeemability does the heavy lifting) |
| Pricing | **All-in** (fees + risk premium baked into the locked rate) |
| OpenRouter | **Neutral across aggregators**; OpenRouter is fulfillment-for-now, stay portable |
| Crypto / Hyperliquid | Returns as a **spot market in asset-backed vouchers**, not a synthetic perp |
