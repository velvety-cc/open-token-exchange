## Tier 1 — make-or-break (answer first)

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="Q1 · Is there enough price movement to trade at all?">
The research never measured **short-horizon volatility or reprice frequency** of OpenRouter
per-model prices. If models reprice rarely and barely move between, there is no active market — only
occasional bulk-buying.

**Decides:** whether OTE is a *trading venue* or merely a *forward/voucher shop*.
**How to resolve:** the Phase-0 index *is* the experiment — but for the plan, you need a prior:
*do you believe token prices move enough intra-quarter to trade, or are you betting purely on
price-lock demand?*
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Q2 · What's the regulatory posture and jurisdiction?">
US treatment of a cash-settled token-price derivative is **unresolved** and the perpetual-futures
perimeter is contested (CME v. CFTC).

**Decides:** whether cash settlement / perps are even on the table, and where users can be.
**Options:** (a) physical-only, avoid the perimeter; (b) B2B cash forwards with ECP gating + counsel;
(c) non-US / geofenced perp. *Which appetite are you building for?*
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Q3 · Who is the first customer, and what do they actually buy?">
Demand-side willingness-to-pay is **unverified**. Candidate segments: high-burn AI startups, agent
platforms, traders, bulk buyers — each wants a different instrument.

**Decides:** the MVP's exact shape (price-lock vs prepaid voucher vs cap/option).
**Need:** a concrete first-customer hypothesis to design the v0 against.
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Tier 2 — product shape

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="Q4 · Per-model or model-class as the primary unit?">
Trade `OTE-GPT5` specifically, or a class like `OTE-FRONTIER` / `OTE-CHEAP-BULK`? Classes are more
liquid and robust to any single model dying; per-model is more precise for a buyer hedging a specific
dependency. Probably offer both, but **which leads the MVP?**
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Q5 · Raw price or SIT-capability-normalized as the headline index?">
The raw "cheapest capable model" series is what buyers pay; the SIT-normalized series is a cleaner
tradeable underlying. **Which is the flagship**, and is the other a secondary view?
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Q6 · Is the HIP-3 perp in scope at all?">
The perp is the riskiest, most-regulated, last phase. Is OTE ultimately a **two-sided trading
exchange** (perp belongs) or a **pure hedging/price-lock venue** (perp may never belong)? This
reframes the whole north star.
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Q7 · Physical settlement economics — who eats the fee layer?">
Physical redemption runs through OpenRouter's 5.5% credit + 5% BYOK fees. Does the **buyer** pay it
(thinner value prop), does **OTE** absorb it (margin), or does the lock price **bake it in**?
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Tier 3 — build & trust

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="Q8 · How many price sources before settlement trusts the index?">
OpenRouter alone is a single-venue dependency. **Minimum source count** and which (other aggregators?
direct provider rate cards?) before the index is allowed to settle money?
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Q9 · Self-run vs third-party oracle (if on-chain)?">
For a perp, self-running `SetOracle` concentrates trust badly. Commit to **Pyth / RedStone** from day
one of Phase 3, or evaluate self-operation? (Recommendation: never self-run.)
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Q10 · OpenRouter dependency & ToS risk">
OTE leans heavily on OpenRouter for both **pricing data** and **physical fulfillment**. Is there ToS
risk in reselling credits / building a market on their prices? Is a **direct-provider** fallback
needed? Does OpenRouter become a partner, a dependency, or a competitor?
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Q11 · Trust & governance of the index">
A trusted index is the moat. Does it need an **independent methodology committee**, third-party
audit, or licensing to a regulated venue (the CME/ICE model) to be credible — or is open,
reproducible computation enough?
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Still OPEN

<Warning>
- **Q12 (NEW) · Collateral / reserve model.** Since tokens can't be inventoried, holder trust in
  redemption depends on **how much capital backs outstanding vouchers, where it's held, and what
  happens when an upward reset exceeds reserves.** A first-class design constraint —
  [Instrument](/architecture/instrument#collateralization-a-first-class-requirement).
- **Q2′ · Securities/commodities counsel.** A *transferable, on-chain, prepaid claim* people may buy
  to resell at a profit needs a real legal read (utility token vs investment contract vs commodity).
  The redeemable/utility anchor helps, but get the memo before the secondary market goes live.
- **Q1′ · Empirical reset study.** The Phase‑0 measurement of decline-forecastability and reset
  frequency/size is still unrun — it gates how aggressive the underwriting discount can be.
</Warning>
