## The bet

> Inference cost is becoming a **material, recurring line item** for AI companies, yet there is no
> instrument to **lock it in, hedge it, or price it forward**. OTE provides that instrument as a
> **prepaid, redeemable token-voucher** — pay tomorrow's lower price today, then redeem for real
> inference or sell to exit.

This reframes the project from a *speculation venue* to a **cost-risk-management venue** — closer
to a fuel-hedging desk (or AWS Reserved Instances) than a meme-coin exchange. Speculation is a
*liquidity by-product* that arrives via the voucher's secondary market, not the core value. The
instrument is specified in [The Core Instrument](/architecture/instrument); how OTE bears and
sheds the risk is in [Recommendation](/architecture/recommendation).

## Why the original framing breaks

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="“Arbitrage token prices” — not possible in the riskless sense">
A token is **consumed at the moment of generation**. You cannot buy 1M tokens cheap on Provider A
and resell them on Provider B. The cross-provider price dispersion that exists (>3× spread for the
same model — see [Is it speculatable?](/research/is-it-speculatable)) is **cost-minimizing routing
of your own consumption**, which OpenRouter already does for you. There is no resellable spread.
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="“Speculate on token prices” — weak because moves are one-directional">
Capability-normalized token prices fell **~40× from 2023→2025**. That is a *secular decline*, not
*bidirectional volatility*. A two-sided speculative market needs prices that surprise in both
directions. A short-biased hedging product (buyers want protection against the rare *up* move; the
trend funds the short side) is the more honest shape.
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="“Bulk-purchase / lock-in” — this one survives">
Locking a forward price for a model or model-class is a **real, defensible product**. It is a
forward/voucher, it sidesteps the need for bidirectional volatility, and it maps onto a genuine
buyer need (budget predictability). This becomes the spine of the recommended MVP.
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Non-goals

<Warning>
These are explicitly **out of scope** for the MVP and should not creep back in:

- **Riskless arbitrage** of token prices (structurally impossible — tokens are consumed).
- **A non-redeemable, cash-settled derivative** as the *first* product — it would reintroduce the
  oracle-manipulation and CFTC exposure the [redeemable voucher](/architecture/instrument) avoids.
  (A perp may come in Phase 3, on top of the anchored spot market — see
  [HIP‑3 path](/architecture/hip3-perp).)
- **A naked, uncollateralized underwriting book.** OTE carries a real short; it must be backed by a
  [reserve/collateral model](/architecture/instrument#collateralization-a-first-class-requirement).
- **Lock-in to OpenRouter.** OTE is a [neutral layer across aggregators](/architecture/system-architecture);
  OpenRouter is fulfillment-for-now, not the foundation.
</Warning>

## Demand-side hypothesis (unverified)

The research did **not** confirm willingness-to-pay. These are the assumptions the MVP must test:

| Segment | Hypothesized need | Instrument |
|---|---|---|
| AI startups w/ large inference bills | Budget predictability for 1–4 quarters | Fixed-price token forward / voucher |
| Agent platforms (high token burn) | Protection against a provider price hike | Cap / call option on token price |
| Traders / market-makers | A new uncorrelated underlying | Cash-settled index future or perp |
| Bulk buyers | Volume discount via committed demand | Physically-settled prepaid voucher |

<Info>
The single most important unanswered question is **short-horizon volatility and reprice frequency**
— if OpenRouter reprices a given model only every few months, an *active* market has nothing to
trade between repricings. This is the first thing the [Open Questions](/architecture/open-questions)
interrogates.
</Info>
